Many date validation programs do not take into account illegal dates such as 30th february and simply do a regex check. This is my answer to this frequent issue (nb: does not include the regex).

Simply splitting the component parts of the date, creating a new date object, and checking the day, month and year match the original is all that is required - as a js date object will automatically increment if an illegal value is entered. Eg. 32nd January will become 1st february - thus the date and month will no longer match. Simple :)
